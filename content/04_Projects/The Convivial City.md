## Overview
"As a city marketing organisation, we are
responsible for promoting the city. So you
might expect that we would formulate a new
plan for attracting more talent and tourists
to Eindhoven. That is not what this strategic
document has become. ==What lies before you
is a proposal for a new vision for the city of
Eindhoven as a whole. A plan that extends
well beyond the scope of our organisation."==

"the great paradox of our time
is that economic growth goes hand in hand
with depletion of the earth’s resources and it
also leaves parts of society suffering poverty."

How can we include everyone? What kind
of city do we want to pass onto future
generations? As Eindhoven’s city marketing
organisation, we want to make a meaningful
contribution to tackling issues.

Where residents, institutions
and businesses can all contribute in their
own way. ==We see five key challenges for the
coming decade: unravelling the social fabric,
magnet for bright talent, climate change
is not a choice, the paradox of scale and
mission-driven connecting.==

Our first five years were focused on developing the city’s brand and its marketing strategy. This is when ==we developed the vibes, Eindhoven’s logo that depicts the city’s energy.== The vibes have been open source from the beginning: free to use for anyone. The city council has chosen to employ it as an official logo for all of its communication, local entrepreneurs incorporate the vibes in their house style, and a number of real Eindhoven fans have immortalised it with a tattoo on their biceps.

These three groups were selected based upon research
investigating what successful cities were doing: attracting tourists, talent and tech companies. From this, we distilled three target groups, being ==City Explorers, Bright Talents and Tech Starters.== Using buyer persona research, which is marketing speak for gaining insight into customer motivation and needs, we mapped the best ways to reach and cater to the needs of these groups.

The outbreak of coronavirus in early 2020 meant, [...] that  we ==shifted our focus temporarily from international to local==. We highlighted many social initiatives, ==promoted countless take-away dinners== and supported our ==local retailers where we could.== Our major awareness campaign ‘Keep our city strong’ promoted the ‘buying local’ to a wide audience. It was a challenging, but very educational period, and ==we did our best to contribute to the local system. Even though the world could get back to ‘normal’ at the drop of a hat, local action will remain fundamental in our thoughts.==

==Eindhoven’s ambition was and remains to be part of the top three creative innovation regions in Europe,== and we are well on our way. The European Commission’s Cultural and Creative Cities Monitor even proclaimed ==Eindhoven ‘Europe’s most innovative city’ in 2019.==

In the years ahead, ==Eindhoven wants to continue to play a leading role on the international stage of creative innovation. ==The playing field on which cities compete for technology, talent and tourists is not static but extremely dynamic. ==Stagnation really equals decline here==


With the growth and ==increasing internationalisation of the city, we see that the different worlds in Eindhoven need more connection==. It is these two worlds that typify the city: ==international and local, theoretically trained and practically schooled, residents born and bred in Eindhoven and newcomers, bright minds and grafters.== However, ==the two worlds of local and international only meet in a few places in the city.== This is a shame because sharing knowledge, meeting up and inspiring each other can foster mutual understanding, recognition and friendship. But also to develop together, learn from and inspire each other. This inclusion makes everyone feel that they are part of a larger community.

==The enormous success of high tech and design benefit the city but could contribute even more to the development and connection of Eindhoven’s community.==

We could also ask ourselves if Eindhoven, in continuing with the ‘shop metaphor’, has not left the shop’s back door open, allowing ==many recently attracted groups to exit unnoticed. And as they leave, do they leave disinterested and frustrated, or as ambassadors for Eindhoven, forever emotionally attached to this city?==

How can we ensure that everyone in Eindhoven gets to share the city’s energy? Here we see a possible fourth task for our organisation: ==the local system’s connector.== That is our promise for the next four years.

## Global Challenges
• Solving complex social issues in areas such as climate and social equality requires ==purpose-driven leadership, with human well-being at the heart of policymaking.==

Many problems originated in cities, and cities will need to solve them. For younger generations, the environment is the first, second and third priority. Governments can no longer ignore climate issues. ==A healthy mind needs a healthy body, and healthy, vibrant people live in a healthy city that prioritises radical climate-centred progress.==

==Cities need to strike a better balance between long-term objectives and the short-term perspective of fighting for political gains.== For governments, a solution could be to not simply own the challenge, but to take it on, as co-creator and partner, with their ecosystem. Such as in the form of a non-governmental initiative that encourages open innovation for a green, healthy, liveable environment, and can operate partly independently of the political discourse.

• ==Digitalisation== will continue to support us in the next ten years to improve our daily lives, but we need to come up with an answer to the downsides of technological progress.

• The world is individualising, and different forms of social exclusion are lurking. ==Societies should feel morally obliged to be inclusive and work together to find solutions to prevent polarisation.==

• The battle for talent is greater than ever, particularly bright individuals seeking solutions to social issues. ==Innovation hubs that give space to these creative innovators will be the winners.==

This mentality is in Eindhoven’s lifeblood. ==In the 1930s, Gilles Holst ran the internationally distinguished Philips Natlab on the basis of multidisciplinary thinking; he allowed the bright talents of the day to embrace anarchy over order and structure.== And it’s precisely this type of thinking that birthed the world’s greatest innovations. ==How do we, as a city, build today’s new multidisciplinary Natlab communities that unite the human sciences and the power of technology?==

## Local Challenges
• Eindhoven is increasing in size and becoming more international. ==Its success is widely acknowledged, but not everyone is benefiting equally from the city’s prosperity==, and they feel distanced from this success.

• Scale has been our mantra since the industrial era, ==but we’ve now reached the point where scale has become a paradox.== Scale is destroying us, yet we need scale to live – and survive – together efficiently. What does scale mean for cities? Is urban growth still the hallowed goal, or is there an ideal scale? Cities have often focused blindly on growth, but what if growth doesn’t have to be the end goal, and can make way for quality of life and impact? ==Will a new international playing field emerge in which Eindhoven might have the ideal scale? With the ultimate balance of compactness in size (quality of life and connectedness) versus big in impact (innovation and prosperity).==

• Eindhoven wants to play a role on the world stage of creative innovation. ==An enormous amount of new talent is required for this, talent that is attracted by a tolerant, lively and cultural community==. Despite recent injections of funding, ==the city’s facilities are not really on a par with its great ambitions.==

• Besides attracting talent, ==Eindhoven needs to focus more clearly on retaining newcomers.== Assisting with the integration of internationals is an essential aspect. ==How should we go about giving these people a home-from-home feeling and involve them more in Eindhoven as a whole?==

• ==Technology and design are still two separate systems in Eindhoven, resulting in the enormous potential for innovation going to waste.== Add to this the fact that the value of design as a sector remains underexposed and opportunities for synergy are left undisturbed. Eindhoven has to seek synergy if it wants to maintain its position as a creative innovation hub.

• Eindhoven has to make waves in ==making tourism more sustainable. On the one hand, to contribute to a better climate and on the other to cater to the changing expectations from tourists.==

## Opportunities
• ==Medium-sized cities seem to be gaining popularity among younger generations, talents and businesses.== Because of their compact size, ==they are more personal, liveable, and have better networks.== This leads to a free exchange of ideas and stronger social coherence.

• ==Green and circular thinking== is the new norm for cities. Many problems arose on the scale of the city, and will have to be solved on the same scale. ==A bridge must be built between creativity and technology to provide radical solutions.==

• Cities must place major ==challenges at the heart of their vision and strategy and connect their ecosystems in a mission-driven manner to come up with new solutions.==

• Social cohesion is crucial for an inclusive city. ==The well-being, happiness and inclusivity of all its people should be at the heart of a city’s ambition.== Diversity enriches a city’s culture.

• Culture and creativity inspire the vitality and imaginative power needed for new inspiration.


We need to build a stronger bridge between technology and social design. Albert Einstein once said: ‘We can’t solve a problem with the same thinking that created it’. Today’s ==social issues require radical thinking. That is exactly where bright talents shine.== They have a holistic vision and, as such, can knit technology and design together. ==A city with ambition sets mission-driven goals and weaves its creative and technological ecosystems together to achieve them==

In the not too distant future, ==there will be far-reaching changes in our world as the use of artificial intelligence increases in our daily lives. Self-driving cars, intelligent houses and self-thinking devices.== In Eindhoven, ==we have invested heavily in artificial intelligence and with central government funding we have established a top institute, called Eindhoven Engine,== to advance this technology. It is clear that the institute was founded from a technological perspective, but ==wouldn’t it be fantastic if Eindhoven also created space here for artists, ethicists, and designers, following in the footsteps of MIT==. 

If we are honest about it, the ==technology side of the city is dominant==, with the design side often remaining underexposed. This is confirmed in conversations with stakeholders, and this is the sentiment in the local design community. ==Of course, in Eindhoven, technology has an economic impact many times greater than that of design,== but as previously mentioned, ==creative impact is just as relevant, although it is not always as easy to express in terms of money.== As a city, we should give designers and makers more attention in order to restore the balance between T and D.

## Intro
"The NRE site, which has been closed until now, has a long history as a former gas factory site on the Eindhovensch Kanaal. Since its foundation in 1899, the area has undergone many transformations. At the moment, the NRE site is on the verge of being transformed into a small-scale, lively and creative area on the edge of the city centre. Not an ordinary city district, but ==a unique place for private individuals, entrepreneurs and other parties who want to jointly create a 'new' and inspiring part of Eindhoven.==

![[Pasted image 20221204162415.png]]

The supervision of the entire process surrounding the redevelopment of the NRE site was designed by the municipality of Eindhoven, with the [[Bauhütte]] providing insights into the history during the various meetings, investigating the development potential of the area and fulfilling an independent role in the translation of proposed ambitions in spatial principles. The Sounding Board Group (including Werkgroep Picuskade/NRE, Van Abbestichting and [[Trefpunt Groen]], among others) has had the opportunity to provide input, share specific knowledge and to reply to visions and plans.

## CORE VISION / A NEW ENERGY
	The NRE site has been inextricably linked to the concept of energy since the first factories were built for the production of gas in 1899. Later, in addition to the production of coal gas, electricity and water gas were also produced. Even after the 1930s, when people switched	from production to distribution, the area went through many transformations. However, the concept of energy has always been a common thread through all developments in the area.

## AMBITION 1 / COLLECTIVE
The scale and nature of the task make it possible to realize
ambitions together. ==By operating collectively and by making direct connections, one can individually benefit from an integrated approach. In addition, a collective approach promotes unity within the site== and the appearance of the NRE site as a whole.
	• Creating and managing public space together.
	• Joint responsibility for a liveable environment.
	• A collective traffic facility (yard structure with one main access).
	• Parking facilities/solutions suitable for the site (dual use where possible).
	• Connecting to each other and the existing (coherence in appearance, material and scale).
	• Collective generation and provision of energy and water (eg: sharing solar panels/water sources).
	• An open appearance; ie a hospitable and at the same time oriented atmosphere.

![[Pasted image 20221204222916.png]]

## AMBITION 2 / INDUSTRIOUS & DYNAMIC
A ==great diversity of functions, residents and activities ensures that an energetic urban neighborhood is developing== in the vicinity of the center of Eindhoven. An ==area where both visitors and new initiatives are more than welcome.==
	• Lively and vital urban living environment.
	• A rich and varied composition of residents, functions, activities and target groups.
	• Initiating and stimulating (temporary) activities (eg: workshops, catering, area design).
	• Involvement of and interaction between residents and users.
	• A phased and organic area development with an open attitude towards new plans and	initiatives.
	• Informal and active use of the (public) space as an extension of the building.

![[Pasted image 20221204222936.png]]

## AMBITION 3 / Historic industrial character
The industrial past is a source of inspiration for new developments. In addition, ==the history of the NRE site is kept
visible and recognizable== and made.
	• New building volumes take up a position that restores the lost connections between existing buildings and, if possible, even strengthens them (see the BAAC report).
	• A relationship between the former harbor head and the NRE site; bringing back and making a water and quay experience readable again.
	• When repurposing existing building volumes, take into account the possibilities and qualities of the	existing building. In other words: choose the function and/or shape that is most suitable for the relevant building.
	• Strengthening a dialogue between existing and new (no anonymous building volumes).
	• New construction takes into account the monumental character of the existing buildings.
	• Coherence in scale, orientation, spatial relations and footprint between existing and new.
	• All-roundness and simplicity of building volumes.
	• Peripheral development follows the border of the NRE site and forms a face to the city.
	• Diversity in typology and physical readability of new developments.

![[Pasted image 20221204222953.png]]

## Transformation Drivers
At an urban level, the small-scale industry on the Eindhovensch
Kanaal does not compare to the large scale and proportions of the former Philips factory sites. In the vicinity of the railway, these industrial areas have left an unmistakable footprint in the urban fabric on the west side of the centre. In addition, the relatively rapid departure of Philips in the 1990s caused this and ensured that large and closed factory sites became available in a
relatively short time. The east side of the center, on the other hand, follows a more gradual and unpredictable path. Since the transport function of the canal was lost in the 1970s, lot by lot has been released.
In contrast to the areas on the west side of the centre, ==these
developments were and still are not based on integrated (area) visions.== The plots around the Eindhovensch Kanaal are all located in or against existing neighborhoods and are therefore also a ==complex playing field of parties and interests==.

The ==planning method of predicting needs after which
the estimated number of square meters is developed
systematically no longer fits in with the current zeitgeist==. In
addition, the inner-city location of the NRE site creates a complex (horizontal) playing field of interests and logical limitations. In keeping with the spatial context on the one hand and the current economic and social trend on the other, ==there is every reason to develop sites on a small-scale, modest and cooperative basis with plenty of room for initiatives and flexibility.== Looking for a new balance in making the city.

### 2027 | Veiligheidshuis: 
Team: Francesca Tambussi, Sichao Yang, Meander van der Weijst

In 2022, this Gemeente building is responsible for improving the safety and quality of life in Southeast Brabant, wraps around the corner of NRE and faces the canal.

In an Eindhoven with rising homelessness following the influx of high-earning tech workers, squats and tent communities form at the various gemeente buildings.

For their 2027 vision, the group in question decided to imagine what the negotiation between a gemeente worker and a young student living in the squat asking to be allowed to stay inside the gemeente building.

Over the course of the discussion they debate number of beds, rebrand the term squatting to “fair use”, and the checking of IDs for those accessing the services.

The negotiation ends when the representative from the side of the squatters agrees to make a promotional video for the city on how helpful it has been towards them.

### 2032 | Gasfabriek 6
Team: Philippe Rol, Yu Zhang, Christina Papadaki
In 2022, the Gasfabriek 6 is a multi-use space with creative organisations and the archeological museum.

In their 2032 vision, the group imagined the building might have some type of intelligence or presence, which ensures all who come through the space understand the they must contribute and take care of the community of the building.

This in no means imply a high-tech future but rather one where commoning and resource distribution have become more common after an important push by the city since 2022 to emphasize the importance of co-creative city making.

In the scene, the group imagined members of the community, spending a pleasant moment by the camp fire, while welcoming a new member and getting to understand the needs and expectations.

"A beautiful future is just sitting around the fire." was their closing remark.

### 2037 | Residential areas
Team: Evelien Nieuwenburg, Leif Czakai, Rene Pare

In 2022, the residential areas of the NRE are composed of different smaller self-organized groups of residents who, in 2017, contributed to the redesign of the area. One of the big decisions taken by the residents at the time was to have open parking spaces on the ground floor of most habitations.

By 2037, following a drastic loss in biodiversity, and brutal heatwaves in the summer, the Gasfabriek has been left to fend for itself after the city of Eindhoven lost interest in it. In response, many residents have self-organized survival hacker groups. 

Together they have been working on various hacks such as "cooling houses" which use repurposed cars' AC units fitted on top of containers to create temperate areas to grow vegetables in.
